Key events

April 20, 2026

First suspected death — health worker, Bunia, Ituri Province, DRC

April 24, 2026

First known suspected case (health worker) reports onset of symptoms including fever, hemorrhaging, and vomiting

May 5, 2026

WHO alerted of high-mortality outbreak of unknown illness in Mongbwalu Health Zone; deaths among healthcare workers

May 15, 2026

Bundibugyo virus confirmed by INRB. DRC officially declares 17th Ebola outbreak. 246 suspected cases, 80 deaths reported across Rwampara, Mongbwalu, and Bunia health zones.

May 16, 2026

WHO Director-General declares PHEIC. Uganda confirms 2 imported cases from DRC in Kampala, including 1 death. No approved vaccine or treatment exists for Bundibugyo virus.

May 18, 2026

US CDC and DHS implement enhanced travel screening and entry restrictions. Risk to US public remains low. A medical missionary evacuated from DRC to Germany for treatment.

May 19–20, 2026

WHO confirms ~600 suspected cases and 139 deaths. Outbreak now spans 11 health zones in Ituri and North Kivu provinces. WHO Emergency Committee confirms PHEIC status at meeting in Geneva.

Data caveat: All case and death figures are suspected/reported totals. The true number of infections likely considerably exceeds official estimates due to the remote location, ongoing armed conflict in Ituri, limited laboratory capacity, and high population mobility in the region. Early pre-declaration figures (Apr 20, May 5) are partially reconstructed from reporting. Sources: WHO Disease Outbreak News (DON602), CDC Situation Summary, Al Jazeera / WHO press conference May 20, 2026.
